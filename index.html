Six Habitats, Six Communities

Different habitats have different conditions — and each supports a different group of organisms.

A habitat is the place where organisms live. It provides the conditions they need: air, water, food, space, and shelter. Because each habitat has different conditions — how wet, how bright, what kind of soil — each one supports a different community. Tap each habitat to explore.

Interactive · Explore each habitat
Why habitats differ
Every habitat has a unique mix of sunlight, temperature, water, and soil. Only organisms that can survive those exact conditions thrive there — which is why you won't find a frog on a beach or a coconut tree in a pond.

Everything Depends on Everything

Plants, fungi, animals, and humans all affect each other's survival.

All the populations in a habitat are interdependent — they rely on each other in ways that are not always obvious. Plants make food for everyone. Fungi and bacteria clean up dead matter. Animals spread seeds and pollinate. Humans can either protect a habitat or destroy it. Choose a group below to see its role.

The pond chain reaction
Too many floating plants block sunlight → fully-submerged plants can't photosynthesise enough → not enough oxygen or food is produced → the pond's animals die.

When Things Get Bad

Living things have three options when their habitat changes.

When environmental conditions become unfavourable — a drought, a flood, a sudden temperature change, or loss of food — organisms respond in one of three ways. They can adapt, die, or migrate.

Three possible responses
🦎
Adapt
Change to survive with the new conditions.
Example: A fox growing thicker fur when winters get colder.
💀
Die
Be completely killed off by the new conditions.
Example: Fish dying when a pond runs out of oxygen.
🦅
Migrate
Leave the habitat to seek a new one with more favourable conditions.
Example: Birds flying south when winter arrives.

Chapter summary
Living things are never alone. Every organism is part of a population, every population is part of a community, and every community depends on the conditions of its habitat. When conditions change, organisms must adapt, migrate, or die. Understanding these relationships is the key to understanding life.
